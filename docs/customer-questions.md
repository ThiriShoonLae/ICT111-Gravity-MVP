# Customer Discovery Questions

## Project Title
Gravity — A Peer-to-Peer Student Marketplace

## Target Respondent
Rangsit University students who have recently bought or sold used items around moving in or out of university — including graduating/leaving students, new and continuing students, international students, and dorm residents.

## Consent and Context
1. May we ask you a few questions for our ICT111 course project?
2. Your participation is voluntary. We will not collect unnecessary personal data. Is it okay if we take anonymized notes?

## Recent Experience
3. Tell us about the last time you tried to sell, give away, or buy a used item around moving in or out of university.
4. What were you trying to do at that time — clear out items, or find something you needed?
5. Walk us through what happened from start to finish.

## Current Workaround
6. How do you usually buy or sell used items now?
7. What apps, groups, websites, or people do you currently use to do this?
8. What is difficult, slow, confusing, or frustrating about the way you do it now?

## Pain Severity and Consequence
9. How often do you buy or sell used items like this?
10. How serious is this problem for you on a scale of 1–5? Why?
11. What happens when you cannot sell an item in time, or cannot find the item you need?

## Closing
12. Is there anything else we should understand about how students buy and sell used items?
13. May we use your anonymized response as part of our course project evidence?

---

### Note on Question Quality
These questions are intentionally **non-leading**. They ask about the respondent's real past behavior, current methods, and consequences — not whether they would like our marketplace. We avoid pitching the solution (for example, we never ask "Would you use our app?") so the evidence reflects the real problem rather than polite approval.
